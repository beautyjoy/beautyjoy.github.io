Guadelupe Valdez

Guadalupe Valdés is the Bonnie and Martin Tenenbaum Professor of Education at Stanford University, and among the nation’s leading researchers in the sociolinguistics of language learning. Her current work in the areas of adult ESL and ESL for low-literacy learners is perfectly aligned with the goals of ESLX.

Bernard Gifford

Bernard R. Gifford is the former Dean of UC Berkeley’s Graduate School of Education, former Vice President for Education at Apple Computer, and an expert in the area of technology-mediated learning and assessment. He was the primary architect of the suite of computer-mediated mathematics lesson materials most frequently used in the nation’s community colleges. He is also a member of the Board of Directors of the Escuela Popular, the charter school located in East San Jose with which ESLX is collaborating.

Adam Mendelson

Adam Mendelson recently completed his doctorate at UC Berkeley’s Graduate School of Education where his dissertation research focused on Computer-Assisted Language Learning, an area in which he has been working for two decades. He is currently involved in teacher training at Sonoma State University where he teaches courses in Educational Technology and Second Language Acquisition to current and future ESL instructors.

Armando Fox

Armando Fox is professor of computer science at UC Berkeley and head of the campuses’ efforts to integrate edX into its core of learning technologies. His unmatched familiarity with edX, as well as his commitment to advancing educational opportunities for Hispanic-origin immigrant students, makes Professor Fox an ideal contributor to ESLX.
